Samosa

Samosa is a popular savory snack that originated in the Indian
subcontinent and has gained worldwide popularity. It consists of a crispy,
triangular-shaped pastry filled with a savory mixture of spiced potatoes, peas,
and sometimes other ingredients like lentils, onions, or minced meat


Samosas are often served hot and are enjoyed as a snack or appetizer, either
on their own or with accompaniments such as tamarind chutney, mint chutney,
or yogurt-based dips. They are also commonly sold by street
vendors and enjoyed at festivals, parties, and gatherings.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Samosa

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Water, as needed
  • 3 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tpsp chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • Oil for frying

How to Make Samosa



To prepare samosa:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and oil or
    ghee. Rub the oil into the flour mixture using your fingertips until it
    resembles breadcrumbs. Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead until
    you have a smooth and firm dough. Cover the dough and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let
    them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.

  3. Add grated ginger and chopped green chilies (if using) to the pan. Sauté for
    a minute until fragrant.

  4. Stir in the boiled mashed potatoes and boiled green peas. Mix well.
    Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt to
    the potato mixture.

  5. Mix until all the spices are well combined. Cook for a few minutes until the ,
    filling is heated through and aromatic.

  6. Remove the pan from heat and let the filling cool down. Once cooled, add
    chopped fresh coriander leaves and mix well.

  7. Divide the rested dough into equal-sized balls. Roll out each ball into a
    thin oval or circle, about 6 inches in diameter.Cut each rolled-out dough
    circle in half to form two semi-circles.

  8. Take one semi-circle and fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edges by
    brushing them with water.Fill the cone with a spoonful of the prepared potato
    filling, making sure not to overfill.

  9. Seal the top of the cone by folding over the remaining dough and pressing
    the edges together to form a triangular shape. Repeat with the remaining dough
    and filling.

  10. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat. Once the oil is
    hot, carefully slide in the assembled samosas, a few at a time, and fry
    until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides.

  11. Remove the fried samosas using a slotted spoon and drain them on paper towels
    to remove excess oil.

  12. Serve the hot and crispy samosas with tamarind chutney, mint chutney, or any
    dipping sauce of your choice. Enjoy your homemade samosas as a delicious snack or appetizer!
